# Workshop Flow

## 1. Warm-up: Listening Scavenger Hunt

**20 minutes**

Give them small tasks inspired by the operator scavenger hunt format. ([digitalideation.github.io][1])

Find a way to:

- make volume control brightness
- make pitch or frequency control shape
- create a visual echo
- make silence visible
- make a voice look old
- make a sound feel hidden
- make noise look like a signal

This gets them into TouchDesigner quickly.

## 2. Main group exercise

**45–60 minutes**

Let each group choose one brief:

1. **Voice Across Time Machine**
2. **Sound as Evidence**
3. **The Hotline**
4. **Listening to the Room**
5. **Collective Voice**

## 3. Micro-presentation

**2 minutes per group**

Each group answers:

- What kind of listening did you choose?
- What does the listener have to do?
- What changed through sound?
- What would you improve with more time?

## 4. Closing discussion

**10 minutes**

Ask:

> **What kind of listening felt most interesting to prototype: intimate, forensic, collective, bodily, environmental, or uncanny?**

# Best exercise to choose

For your specific presentation, I would pick **one of these three**:

## Most poetic

**Voice Across Time Machine**

Best if you want the workshop to connect to memory, archive, old recordings, and voices from the past.

## Most TouchDesigner-friendly

**Listening to the Room**

Best if students are beginners because it works with live mic input and simple audio-reactive visuals.

## Most narrative

**The Hotline**

Best if you want them to think about interaction, interface, voice, and emotional stakes.

My suggestion:
Use **Listening to the Room** as a warm-up, then **Voice Across Time Machine** as the main group exercise.
